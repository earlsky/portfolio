02   EARNINGS BRIEFING TRANSCRIPT
10-Minute Investor Audio Guide
00:00 — INTRODUCTION
Welcome & Context

Welcome to the Bumble Q4 2025 earnings briefing. I'm going to walk you through everything you need to understand from last night's results in about ten minutes. Bumble reported after the close on March 11, 2026. The stock initially spiked as much as 34% in after-hours before settling to close up roughly 17% on heavy volume. The headline story is complex: a big GAAP loss masking genuine operational progress, a revenue top-line beat, and guidance that largely met expectations. Let's break it down.

📍 Investor Context

Bumble entered 2025 having replaced its CEO, with founder Whitney Wolfe Herd returning to the helm. The year was framed as a deliberate "quality reset" — the company voluntarily shrunk its user base by removing fake and low-intent accounts, accepting short-term revenue pain to strengthen the long-term foundation. Q4 2025 is the culmination of that painful transition year.

01:00 — THE HEADLINE NUMBERS
Revenue Beat, EPS Miss — What Actually Happened

Total revenue for Q4 came in at $224.2 million — that beat consensus of approximately $221.7 million by about $2.5 million, or roughly 1.1%. Not a blowout, but a clean beat in a difficult year. The Bumble app itself generated $181 million (vs. ~$179.7M estimated), and Badoo plus other revenue was $43.2 million (vs. ~$42.7M estimated). Both segments beat.

Now the EPS number looks horrifying on the surface: a GAAP loss of $4.06 per share, versus consensus of a $0.24 gain. But here's what you need to know — that loss is almost entirely explained by $630.5 million of non-cash goodwill impairment charges taken in the quarter. These are accounting write-downs of acquired goodwill, not cash leaving the business. The underlying operational performance, measured by Adjusted EBITDA, held steady at $71.6 million — essentially flat with the $72.5 million from Q4 2024 — while the margin actually expanded from 27.7% to 31.9%.

"In 2025, we made the deliberate choice to return Bumble to its women-first foundation, raising the bar on trust and authenticity while addressing pain points our members experience with online dating. With the heavy lift of our quality reset behind us, we are accelerating product innovation and prioritizing member experience enhancements." — Whitney Wolfe Herd, Founder & CEO, Q4 2025 Earnings Call, March 11, 2026
🔑 Key Investor Insight

The $630.5M impairment is non-cash. Adjust for it and the quarterly EPS picture is closer to breakeven on a non-GAAP adjusted basis. The market correctly looked past the GAAP headline and focused on EBITDA quality and cash generation — $59.1 million in Q4 operating cash flow alone, versus negative $5.4 million in Q4 2024.

02:30 — THE USER DECLINE
The Hardest Metric to Defend

Let's not sugarcoat this: total paying users dropped 20.5% year-over-year, from 4.2 million to 3.3 million. The Bumble app specifically lost 22% of its paying user base, down to approximately 2.2 million. Badoo paying users fell 17% to roughly 1.1 million. These are steep declines.

Management's narrative is that this was intentional — they removed accounts that didn't meet quality standards as part of the reset. The counterfactual they're asking investors to believe is that the remaining user base is higher-intent, more trust-worthy, and more monetizable. The ARPPU data supports this: average revenue per paying user rose 7.9% to $22.20 in Q4, which partially offsets the volume loss.

"The sequential stabilization in our business metrics, including active users and payers, has not yet been reflected in our year-over-year revenue growth — potentially delaying visible financial recovery." — Management risk disclosure, Q4 2025 Earnings Call
⚠️ Watch This Carefully

The key question for 2026 is whether paying users stabilize and then re-grow. The company has not yet provided full-year user growth guidance. Investors will need to see Q1 2026 user metrics for the first real test of whether the reset narrative holds up.

04:00 — MARGINS & PROFITABILITY
The EBITDA Story Is Actually Good

For the full year 2025, Adjusted EBITDA rose slightly to $313.6 million from $304 million in 2024 — a 3% increase — even as revenue fell 10%. That means EBITDA margin expanded from 28% to 32.5% for the full year. This is meaningful cost discipline. Selling and marketing spend was cut from $259 million (24% of revenue in 2024) down to $161 million (17% of revenue in 2025). That's nearly $100 million of marketing savings in a single year.

Product development investment, however, increased to $96 million (10% of revenue) from $84 million (8% of revenue) in 2024 — showing the company is redirecting savings toward the product roadmap rather than simply harvesting the business. The full year generated $250.4 million in operating cash flow and $239 million in free cash flow, providing genuine financial flexibility.

💡 Margin Expansion Driver: Direct Billing

A key surprise was the alternative billing initiative. The company saw a full percentage point of gross margin expansion in Q4 from direct billing — bypassing Apple's 30% App Store fee where possible. Apple Pay adoption was described as "very, very rapid." This structural improvement in unit economics is not yet fully priced by consensus models.

05:30 — BALANCE SHEET
Debt Situation: Manageable But Worth Monitoring

At year end, Bumble held $175.8 million in cash against total debt of $588.5 million — net debt of approximately $413 million. The company repaid $25 million of its Term Loan B in August 2025. With $239 million in annual free cash flow, the net debt / EBITDA ratio sits around 1.3x — a serviceable level, though the market cap of roughly $440 million (post earnings pop) means equity holders are looking at a highly leveraged structure relative to size.

06:30 — PRODUCT ROADMAP
AI Matchmaker, BFF Growth, International Expansion

Management devoted significant call time to the product pipeline. Bumble BFF, the friend-finding product, launched group discoverability features that drove a 17% increase in active groups within just two weeks. International expansion and AI-driven matchmaking were highlighted as key 2026 priorities. The CEO described an "AI matchmaker" concept as a core product investment aimed at improving match quality, which would differentiate Bumble from lower-effort swipe-based competitors.

The technology stack is also being rebuilt: CFO Kevin Cook flagged that there will be "some duplication of costs for a portion of 2026" as the company runs both its legacy and new cloud-native infrastructure simultaneously during the transition — a one-time headwind to watch in H1 2026.

08:00 — GUIDANCE
Q1 2026 Outlook — In Line, Not a Catalyst

For Q1 2026, Bumble guided total revenue to $209–$213 million (midpoint $211M), with Bumble App revenue of $171–$174 million and Adjusted EBITDA of $76–$80 million (midpoint $78M, ~37% margin). The revenue midpoint matched analyst consensus of approximately $210.9 million — so no outperformance beat on the forward guide. But the EBITDA guidance was notably strong, implying continued margin expansion even as revenue is still declining year-over-year.

09:15 — THE BOTTOM LINE
What This All Means for Investors

Bumble is a turnaround story at an early and still unproven inflection point. The quality reset year is over, the cost structure has been dramatically improved, and management is optimistic about the product pipeline. The bear case is simple: user declines haven't stopped, revenue is still contracting, and there's no 2026 user growth guidance to anchor to. The bull case is equally straightforward: free cash flow generation is real, margins are expanding structurally, the founder is back with a credible vision, and the stock at roughly $3.82 trades at a deep discount to intrinsic value if any user stabilization materializes. This is a high-risk, high-optionality position — not a core holding for conservative portfolios.

📊 Final Analyst Take

Multiple analysts cut price targets post-earnings (reflecting the user decline and revenue headwinds) while maintaining Hold ratings. The consensus 12-month price target sits around $4.53–$4.65, implying modest upside from the current price. The critical unlock: sequential paying user stabilization in Q1 or Q2 2026 would be a meaningful positive catalyst.

07   INVESTMENT FRAMEWORK
Bull Case vs. Key Risks
🟢 Bull Case
  • ▲Quality reset declared complete — if true, paying user base has found a floor and ARPPU growth can accelerate from a higher-quality cohort
  • ▲Margin expansion is structural: EBITDA margin went from 28% to 32.5% in a revenue-decline year; any revenue recovery adds disproportionate profit upside
  • ▲Direct billing / Apple Pay bypass creates a durable gross margin tailwind — 100bps expansion in Q4 alone with further runway ahead
  • ▲$239M free cash flow generation at a ~$440M market cap implies a ~54% FCF yield — deeply undervalued if growth returns
  • ▲Founder-led turnaround with credible women-first brand differentiation in a market where generic swiping apps face commoditization pressure
  • ▲Bumble BFF group features driving 17% active group growth in two weeks post-launch — potential unlocks a new non-dating TAM
  • ▲Stock at ~$3.82 trades at ~0.45x revenue (EV/Sales) — historically low for any app with 32%+ EBITDA margins and real cash generation
🔴 Key Risks
  • ▼Paying user decline of 20.5% YoY shows no sign of bottoming — if quality reset narrative is wrong and users simply left, revenue may continue falling
  • ▼No full-year 2026 user guidance provided — management is unwilling or unable to commit to when user growth returns
  • ▼$588.5M in debt against $440M market cap means equity is a leveraged, junior claim — any deterioration in cash flow intensifies credit stress
  • ▼Technology stack migration creates H1 2026 cost duplication risk — CFO warned margins may face near-term pressure
  • ▼Multiple analysts cut price targets post-earnings; institutional sentiment remains cautious — limited near-term re-rating catalyst
  • ▼Competitive intensity from Hinge, Tinder (Match Group) and emerging AI-native dating apps could absorb users departing Bumble
  • ▼Macro sensitivity: premium subscription dating apps face churn pressure in weaker consumer spending environments
