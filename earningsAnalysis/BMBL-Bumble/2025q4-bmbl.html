Total paying users fell 11.5% to 3.7M for 2025, but Bumble highlighted stabilization in app registrations and active users even after cutting performance marketing by more than 80%.[web:1]

“We believe the member base reset is largely complete, and we are now positioned to grow from a higher‑quality, more engaged core audience.”[web:1] Q4 2025 prepared remarks

02:30 — profitability & cash
CFO commentary (summarized)[web:1]

Adjusted EBITDA margin expanded to 31.9% in Q4 and 32.5% for the full year, backed by $250.4M in operating cash flow despite a reported net loss of $906.6M for 2025.[web:1]

“Our cash generation and margin profile give us flexibility to invest in Tech Stack 2.0 while maintaining financial discipline.”[web:1] Q4 2025 financial highlights

04:00 — outlook & 2026 plan
Management commentary (summarized)[web:1]

07 • positioning
Bull case vs key risks
Framed for medium‑term, risk‑tolerant investors.[web:1][web:3][web:7]
Upside drivers
Bull case — why own Bumble here?
  • ↗ Valuation embeds a lot of pessimism: a ~$321M market cap against ~$966M in 2025 revenue and >30% adjusted EBITDA margins offers asymmetric upside if growth restarts.[web:1][web:3][web:9]
  • ↗ Tech Stack 2.0, AI personalization, and group‑based features could improve conversion and revive user growth from a higher‑quality base.[web:1]
  • ↗ Strong cash generation ($250.4M from operations in 2025) provides funding for product investment without immediately stressing the balance sheet.[web:1]
  • ↗ Bumble App remains the core profit engine at ~80%+ of revenue, with room to layer new verticals like BFF and Bizz on top.[web:1]
Watch‑outs
Risks — what can go wrong?
  • ↘ Execution risk: if the 2026 relaunch fails to meaningfully accelerate paying user growth, the stock could remain a value trap despite low multiples.[web:1]
  • ↘ Competitive pressure from larger platforms and niche apps may cap pricing power and growth in core dating.[web:1]
  • ↘ Leverage and GAAP losses — $588.5M in debt and a $906.6M 2025 net loss highlight limited room for major missteps.[web:1]
  • ↘ Macro and regulatory uncertainties around online dating, safety, and app‑store policies could disrupt engagement or monetization.[web:1]
