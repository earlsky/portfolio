01 — Earnings Call Briefing
10-Minute Investor Summary
00:00 — Opening / Investor Relations
Doug Clark — VP, Investor Relations
Adobe's Q1 FY2026 earnings call opened after market close on March 12, 2026. The call included Shantanu Narayen (CEO), David Wadhwani (President, Creativity & Productivity), Anil Chakravarthy (President, Customer Experience Orchestration), and Dan Durn (CFO). Management covered the quarter's record financials, AI product momentum, and addressed the CEO succession announcement.
01:00 — CEO Strategic Overview & Succession
Shantanu Narayen — Chair & CEO
Narayen opened with the most consequential personal announcement of his 18-year tenure: he will transition from the CEO role after a successor is identified in partnership with Lead Director Frank Calderoni and the Board. He will remain as Chair to support his successor.

"Adobe delivered record Q1 results with AI-first ARR more than tripling year over year and subscription revenue growing 13 percent. Our mission to empower everyone to create represents an even larger opportunity as content powers all experiences in the AI era."

— Shantanu Narayen, Chair & CEO
Narayen outlined Adobe's AI transformation strategy targeting four customer segments: business professionals, consumers, creative professionals, and marketing professionals — through both AI-infused existing applications and new AI-first standalone products including Adobe Firefly, Acrobat Studio, Adobe Express, and Adobe GenStudio. He highlighted 850M+ monthly active users, up 17% YoY, as the primary monetization pipeline indicator.
🔑 Key Investor Insight

The CEO transition is a significant governance event. Narayen has led Adobe for 18 years and 100 earnings calls, steering revenue from ~$3B to $25B+. Leadership uncertainty, combined with ongoing AI competition concerns, may weigh on multiple until a successor is announced.

02:30 — Creativity & Productivity Deep Dive
David Wadhwani — President, Creativity & Productivity
Business Professionals & Consumers subscription revenue reached $1.78B, growing 15% YoY. Acrobat AI Assistant MAU doubled YoY. Express MAU tripled YoY, now used in 99% of US Fortune 500 companies. Adobe Acrobat Studio — which bundles PDF tools with AI creative capabilities — saw strong upgrade adoption across enterprise renewals. Creative freemium MAU crossed 80 million, up 50% YoY, spanning Firefly, Express, Premiere, Photoshop, and Lightroom across web and mobile surfaces.
Creative & Marketing Professionals subscription revenue was $4.39B, growing 11% YoY (12% in constant currency). 85% of films premiering at the 2026 Sundance Film Festival used Adobe Creative Cloud. Firefly Foundry — Adobe's enterprise content automation engine — is gaining traction in media and entertainment through partnerships with B5 Studios, Creative Artists Agency, and major talent agencies. However, Wadhwani was candid about a headwind: the traditional stock business declined more steeply than expected.
⚠ Headwind: Stock Business Decline

Adobe's legacy stand-alone stock content business (~$450M annual revenue) is declining faster than anticipated. This contributed to ARR growing 10.9% rather than the 10.2% targeted growth rate — a slight beat, but the speed of structural shift from perpetual/stock to subscription/freemium is creating short-term ARR noise.

"Firefly subscription and credit pack ending ARR grew 75% quarter over quarter... generative credit consumption rose 45% and video generative actions grew 8× year over year."

— David Wadhwani, President, Creativity & Productivity
05:00 — Customer Experience Orchestration
Anil Chakravarthy — President, Customer Experience Orchestration
Adobe Experience Platform (AEP) and apps subscription revenue grew over 30% YoY. GenStudio ARR grew over 30% YoY as enterprises deploy AI-driven content supply chains. Adobe serves 99 of the Fortune 100. The platform now processes 35 trillion segment evaluations and 70 billion profile activations per day — infrastructure metrics that underscore enterprise entrenchment. Adobe-powered systems during the 2026 Super Bowl peaked at 8B analytics server hits, 21M concurrent viewers, and delivered 216M emails.
🔑 Enterprise AI Differentiation

AEP's 30%+ growth is the underappreciated gem in this quarter. At scale, enterprise content orchestration is structurally stickier than creative tools, has larger contract values, and is harder for competitors like Canva or Midjourney to replicate. Watch this segment's ARR trajectory as the primary long-term growth driver.

07:30 — Financial Results & Guidance
Dan Durn — EVP & CFO
Total Adobe ending ARR of $26.06B, growing 10.9% YoY. Total subscription revenue of $6.17B, growing 13% YoY. Remaining Performance Obligations (RPO) of $22.22B growing 13% YoY — with 67% current, signaling strong near-term revenue visibility. Cash and short-term investments of $6.89B. The company repurchased approximately 8.1 million shares during the quarter, with $3.89B remaining of its $25B authorization. GAAP operating margin of 37.8%; non-GAAP 47.4%.

"Adobe delivered 13 percent subscription revenue growth and record Q1 cash flow of $2.96 billion. As we accelerate AI-powered capabilities across creativity, productivity and customer experience orchestration, Adobe is well positioned for continued profitable growth."

— Dan Durn, EVP & CFO
Q2 FY2026 Guidance: Revenue $6.43B–$6.48B (midpoint $6.455B, vs. est. $6.43B — slight beat). Non-GAAP EPS $5.80–$5.85 (midpoint $5.825, vs. est. $5.68 — beat). Non-GAAP operating margin ~44.5%. FY2026 full-year targets reaffirmed: Revenue $17.75B–$17.90B, Total Adobe ARR growth 10.2%, Non-GAAP EPS $23.30–$23.50. Note: the pending Semrush acquisition is not included in Q2 or FY26 guidance.
💡 Why Did the Stock Fall Despite the Beat?

Markets have elevated their AI monetization expectations. Despite beating on revenue, EPS, operating margin, and issuing above-consensus guidance, ADBE fell ~6–8% AH. Investors are debating: (1) whether MAU growth translates to ARR in a reasonable timeframe; (2) leadership uncertainty from the CEO transition; (3) the legacy stock business headwind; and (4) whether the 10.2% ARR growth target represents sufficient acceleration in the AI era.

09:30 — Q&A Highlights
Analyst Q&A Summary
Key Q&A themes: (1) CEO succession timeline — management would not provide a specific timeline but emphasized commitment to a smooth transition. (2) ARR headwind from freemium model — Narayen framed high MAU growth as a "phase-shifted" ARR opportunity, analogous to the Reader ubiquity strategy that ultimately drove Acrobat revenue. (3) Semrush integration — pending regulatory approval, expected to close in Q2. (4) AI competitive moat — management cited 850M MAUs, enterprise entrenchment with Fortune 100, Firefly's ethical training data advantage, and deep workflow integration as durable differentiators vs. Canva and Midjourney.

06 — Investment Framework
Bull Case vs. Key Risks
Bull Case
▲AI-first ARR 3× YoY signals early but powerful monetization inflection — freemium MAU base of 850M is pipeline for paid conversion.
▲AEP & GenStudio growing 30%+ YoY with Fortune 100 saturation — enterprise stickiness creates durable moat vs. Canva/Midjourney.
▲47.4% non-GAAP operating margins among the highest in software — exemplary cash generation ($2.96B Q1 OCF).
▲$25B buyback program provides ongoing EPS support; $3.89B remaining authorization as of Q1 end.
▲CEO transition could catalyze multiple re-rating if successor signals accelerated AI revenue strategy.
▲Stock trades at –38% from 1-year ago; analyst average target ~$383–$395 implies 50%+ upside from current levels.
Key Risks
▼CEO succession uncertainty — 18 years of institutional leadership walks out the door; market will demand clarity on successor profile and timeline.
▼Traditional stock business declining faster than expected (~$450M annual run-rate at risk) — structural headwind to near-term ARR.
▼Freemium MAU monetization is "phase-shifted" — high MAUs today don't directly translate to ARR today; market is pricing in skepticism.
▼Canva, Midjourney, OpenAI, and Figma (now independent post-blocked Adobe deal) continue pressing on Creative Cloud's price point and ease of use.
▼10.2% ARR growth guidance for full FY2026 may underwhelm investors expecting AI to drive a step-change acceleration above 15%.
▼Semrush acquisition (pending) adds integration risk and is not included in current guidance; regulatory timing uncertain.
