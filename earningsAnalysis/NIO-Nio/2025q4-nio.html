10-Minute Retail Investor Briefing — Transcript

00:00 — Opening
Welcome — Why This Quarter Is Different

Welcome to this retail investor briefing on NIO's Q4 and full year 2025 financial results, released today — March 10, 2026. If you own NIO stock, are considering it, or simply follow the electric vehicle industry, this is the earnings report you've been waiting for — because something happened today that has never happened before in NIO's eight-year history as a public company.

NIO turned a quarterly profit. For the first time ever. And it wasn't close. The company's earnings per share came in at positive RMB 0.29 — versus analyst expectations of a loss of RMB 0.09. That's a swing of RMB 0.38, and it's one of the biggest beats in NIO's history. Let's break down exactly how it happened, and what it means for investors.

01:00 — Deliveries
Record Deliveries Across All Three Brands

The engine behind NIO's profitability is volume — and this quarter NIO delivered a record 124,807 vehicles, up 71.7% from the same quarter last year and up 43.3% from Q3 2025. December alone saw 48,135 deliveries — up 54.6% year-over-year — capping off the strongest quarter in company history.

Crucially, these deliveries came from all three of NIO's brands:

NIO brand (premium vehicles, 400,000+ RMB): 67,433 units. The flagship All-New ES8 SUV became the top-selling vehicle priced above 400,000 yuan in all of 2025 — meaning it outsold every luxury competitor in that price bracket in China, including some German marques.

ONVO brand (family-oriented, more affordable): 38,290 units. The ONVO L90 became China's best-selling large battery-electric SUV for the entire year. This is significant — it proves NIO can compete outside the ultra-luxury niche.

Firefly brand (premium small cars): 19,084 units. Launched later in 2025, Firefly has already captured the leading position in the premium small car segment since its debut.

For the full year 2025, NIO delivered 326,028 vehicles across all three brands, up 46.9% from 2024. The company crossed the milestone of 1 million cumulative deliveries, reaching 1,045,571 total vehicles as of the end of February 2026.

02:30 — Revenue
Revenue Surged 76% — A Massive Beat

Total Q4 revenue came in at RMB34.65 billion, or approximately $4.95 billion USD. That's a 75.9% increase year-over-year and a 59% jump just from Q3 to Q4 — that Q3-to-Q4 sequential jump is unusually large and reflects the massive ramp in deliveries as all three brands hit their stride simultaneously.

Vehicle sales specifically were RMB31.6 billion, up 80.9% year-over-year, driven by both higher volume and what CFO Stanley Qu called "an optimised product mix" — meaning more high-priced NIO brand vehicles were sold relative to the cheaper ONVO models, lifting average revenue per vehicle. Other sales — battery-swap services, accessories, used cars, technical R&D — came in at RMB3 billion, up 36.6% year-over-year.

For the full year, NIO's total revenues reached RMB87.5 billion (~$12.5 billion USD), up 33% from 2024. Vehicle revenue for the full year was RMB76.9 billion, up 32%.

Revenue Beat Context: NIO's revenue of RMB34.65B came in ahead of the RMB33.25B analyst consensus. This matters because in Q1 and Q2 2025, NIO missed revenue estimates. The Q3 2025 quarter also came in below expectations. So Q4's beat represents a genuine inflection — NIO is now executing ahead of expectations, not behind them.

04:00 — Profitability
The Numbers Behind the First Profit

Let's talk about the margins — because this is where the real story is. Vehicle margin in Q4 hit 18.1%, up from 13.1% a year earlier and 14.7% last quarter. That's a 500 basis point year-over-year improvement. To translate: for every $100 of cars NIO sold, $18.10 was gross profit — versus $13.10 one year ago. The improvement came from three places: scale (more vehicles spreading fixed costs), a better product mix (premium NIO brand outselling cheaper ONVO on a per-unit-revenue basis in Q4), and ongoing cost reductions in batteries and components.

Gross margin for the whole company — including services — reached 17.5%, up from 11.7% in Q4 2024 and 13.9% in Q3 2025. Gross profit was RMB6.07 billion, more than doubling from Q3 and rising 163% year-on-year.

The result: NIO reported an adjusted operating profit of RMB1.25 billion (~$179 million), its first ever. On a GAAP basis, NIO reported an operating profit of RMB807.3 million, also its first. Net income on an adjusted basis was RMB1.23 billion. This is historic. One year ago — Q4 2024 — NIO lost RMB5.5 billion at the adjusted operating level. The company swung by RMB6.75 billion in a single year.

"In the fourth quarter of 2025, our vehicle margin reached 18.1%, and other sales margin reached 11.9%. These improvements were primarily driven by strong delivery and revenue growth, an optimised product mix, and cost reduction and efficiency enhancement initiatives."

— Stanley Yu Qu, CFO, NIO Q4 2025 Earnings Call
05:30 — The Three Brands Explained
NIO's Multi-Brand Strategy — And Why It's Working

To understand NIO's future, retail investors need to understand that NIO is no longer a single-brand company. It operates like a mini automotive group with three distinct brands targeting very different customers.

The NIO brand targets China's affluent buyer — think BMW 7-Series or Mercedes S-Class territory, priced from 300,000–500,000+ yuan ($40,000–$70,000+). It competes directly with German luxury brands and has won market share from them. It's the highest-margin business and NIO's original identity.

The ONVO brand targets middle-class Chinese families — priced from around 150,000 yuan ($21,000), directly competing with BYD's premium models and Tesla's Model Y. The ONVO L90's achievement of becoming China's best-selling large BEV SUV is a stunning commercial success that shows NIO can scale beyond its niche. ONVO is volume; NIO brand is margin.

The Firefly brand targets young, urban professionals who want a premium small car — think Mini Cooper territory but electric and packed with technology. At its price point it's been a hit since launch.

Why this matters for investors: NIO's total addressable market tripled when it launched ONVO and Firefly. The NIO brand alone could never generate the delivery volumes needed for profitability. ONVO and Firefly provide the mass-market scale that makes the economics work. Q4 proved the strategy: volume from ONVO + margin from NIO brand = first-ever profit.

07:00 — Technology & Infrastructure
Battery Swap, 100M Swaps, and Global Expansion

NIO's most unique competitive advantage is its battery-swap network — a system where, instead of waiting 30 minutes to charge, drivers pull into a station and swap their depleted battery for a fully charged one in about three minutes. NIO crossed 100 million battery swaps in 2025 and now operates nearly 3,800 swap stations across China and internationally. This infrastructure creates switching costs — once you own a NIO-compatible vehicle and rely on the swap network, you're unlikely to defect to a competitor who doesn't offer it.

On technology, NIO highlighted continued progress on its "twelve full-stack core technologies" — these span the battery, electric drivetrain, smart cabin, and autonomous driving systems. The company is developing everything in-house rather than buying components from Nvidia or other suppliers, which gives it more control over costs and differentiation over time. This vertical integration is expensive in the short term but is becoming a margin advantage as volumes grow.

Internationally, NIO is expanding its footprint into Central Asia, Australia, New Zealand, and multiple European markets in 2026. The European expansion is gradual but strategic — NIO's premium positioning and battery-swap technology offer something Tesla does not.

08:30 — Guidance & 2026 Outlook
Q1 2026 Guidance Above Consensus — Growth Accelerating

For Q1 2026, NIO guided for total revenue between RMB24.48 billion and RMB25.18 billion — ahead of the analyst consensus of RMB23.3 billion. Vehicle deliveries are guided at 80,000 to 83,000 units, representing year-over-year growth of 90% to 97%.

Early data supports the optimistic guidance. In January 2026, NIO delivered 27,182 vehicles; in February, 27,797 — putting Q1 on track if March can hit roughly 25,000–28,000. Year-to-date 2026 deliveries of 47,979 are already up 77.3% versus the same period last year.

Management has set a long-term vehicle margin target of approximately 20%, supported by upcoming launches of three new premium large SUVs that benefit from the shared battery-swap platform. Reaching 20% vehicle margin with continued volume growth would signal genuine automotive-scale profitability, not just a one-time quarter.

09:30 — The Stock & What to Watch
The Stock: Still Down 92% From Peak — But a Different Company Now

NIO stock remains down roughly 92% from its January 2021 all-time high of $66 — a brutal reminder of how far global EV valuations fell from pandemic-era speculation. The stock closed at $4.94 yesterday and is trading approximately 7.7% higher in pre-market at ~$5.32 today.

Analyst consensus price targets hover around $6.95, suggesting meaningful upside from today's pre-market price if NIO can sustain profitability. The median analyst rating is a moderate buy, though several analysts are in wait-and-see mode to confirm that Q4's profit isn't a one-quarter anomaly.

The key risks remain real: NIO carries significant debt from its infrastructure investments; competition from BYD, Tesla, Huawei-backed Aito, and dozens of Chinese EV startups is intense; and the Chinese EV market is subject to government policy shifts and potential tariff complications for European expansion. The full-year 2025 operating loss was still RMB14 billion — profitability is a Q4 2025 achievement, not yet a full-year story.

But for investors who believed in the multi-brand strategy, the battery-swap moat, and NIO's technology bet, today's results are the clearest validation yet. The company is growing fast, margins are expanding, and it turned profitable — exactly as promised.

10:00 — Key Takeaways
Four Things to Remember

First: NIO reported its first-ever quarterly non-GAAP operating profit, RMB1.25 billion, ending more than a decade of quarterly losses. This is the single most important datapoint from today's report.

Second: The multi-brand strategy worked. ONVO's volume scale and NIO brand's pricing power combined to drive vehicle margins to 18.1% — the highest in company history and approaching the 20% target.

Third: Guidance for Q1 2026 beat consensus, and early delivery data for January and February is on track. The growth story isn't decelerating.

Fourth: The stock is still deeply discounted from its peak and is inexpensive relative to revenue. The question is no longer can NIO be profitable — it just was. The question now is: can it stay profitable? That's a much better question to be asking.
